Bahia Palace: The Brilliance of the Grand Vizier

"A masterpiece intended to be the greatest palace of its time, capturing the very soul of Moroccan craftsmanship."

The Bahia Palace, whose name translates literally to "Brilliance," is a stunning 19th-century complex located in the heart of Marrakech. Conceived originally by Si Moussa, the grand vizier of Sultan Muhammad IV, its construction continued under his son, Ba Ahmed, who expanded the site into the sprawling architectural marvel seen today.

Built to showcase the absolute peak of Islamic and Moroccan architectural styles, the palace is a labyrinth of rooms, courtyards, and gardens. Every surface tells a story: from the intricate zellige tilework patterns to the hand-carved cedar wood ceilings and the delicate stucco work that adorns the walls of the private quarters.
