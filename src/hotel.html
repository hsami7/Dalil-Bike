A legendary palace that breathes the very soul of Marrakech since 1923.

Nestled within eight hectares of ancient olive groves and manicured gardens, La Mamounia is not merely a hotel; it is an enduring symbol of Moroccan hospitality and timeless elegance. Opened in 1923, this grand dame of Marrakech has hosted kings, presidents, and icons of the arts for a century.

Winston Churchill famously called it "one of the most beautiful places in the world," often spending his afternoons painting the Atlas Mountains from his balcony. The architecture is a breathtaking symphony of Moorish-Andalusian traditions and Art Deco flourishes, curated to perfection.
